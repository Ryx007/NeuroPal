Chapter 3 · Quantum Imaging
Correlation-Plenoptic Imaging with Entangled Photons

Sub-shot-noise imaging exploits photon-number correlations that have no classical analogue. In the entangled regime, the joint detection probability factorizes only up to a correlation term, so the biphoton amplitude carries the spatial information of both arms simultaneously.

Writing the two-photon state for orthogonal polarizations $H$ and $V$, the spatial wavefunction is antisymmetric:

$$\Psi(\mathbf r_a,\mathbf r_b)=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}\big[\psi_H(\mathbf r_a)\psi_V(\mathbf r_b)-\psi_V(\mathbf r_a)\psi_H(\mathbf r_b)\big]$$

The measured correlation visibility, with singles rates $P_a$ and $P_b$ and coincidence rate $P_{ab}$, is

(3.14) $$\mathcal V=\frac{P_{ab}-P_aP_b}{\sqrt{P_aP_b}},\qquad \langle\hat n_a\hat n_b\rangle-\langle\hat n_a\rangle\langle\hat n_b\rangle>0.$$

Because the correlation term survives even when each arm is separately incoherent, the plenoptic reconstruction beats the standard quantum limit by a factor set by the Schmidt number of the source.
